Declaration of Independence
The most important document of Early American literature.

Claim

Happiness and Freedom

The Declaration of Independence is the most important document of Early American literature as it effectively sums up centuries worth of sentiments surrounding happiness and freedom, notably seen in its incorporation of themes common to Puritan and non-Puritan works, even including Native American values that existed long before the arrival of the English.

Support

Puritan values of happiness

Tight-knit community

  • Puritans believe happiness is derived from helping others in the community
  • They are fated by God to succeed, to create a utopian "city on a hill", so long as they remain together
  • Declaration of Independence shows community, teamwork
  • 13 states join together to fight off a common enemy - the British
  • Fighting for freedom together results in happiness
knit more nearly together in the bonds of brotherly affection. We must delight in each other; make others’ conditions our own; rejoice together, mourn together, labor and suffer together, always having before our eyes our commission and community in the work, as members of the same body.
John Winthrop's "Dreams of a City on a Hill"

Declaration of Independence

Counterargument

But Native American literature is marginalized and not represented at all in the Declaration of Independence!

Rebuttal

Values of happiness and freedom in Native American literature is reflected in the Declaration of Independence as well.

Gratitude

But Crow told them that the daylight would not last forever. Then they thanked Crow over and over again.
Crow Brings the Daylight
We must, therefore, acquiesce in the necessity, which denounces our separation, and hold them, as we hold the rest of mankind, enemies in war, in peace friends.
Declaration of Independence

Conclusion

  • The Declaration of Independence combines elements of happiness and freedom from all genres of Early American literature.
  • The Declaration of Independence should be preserved for its ability to capture the core values of the time and its contribution to what will become the American Dream.
